CWAD

A framework for clean whisper speech extraction

Pablo Pérez Zarazaga, Zofia Malisz, Gustav Eje Henter

Summary

The goal of this work is to develop a framework for whisper activity detection (WAD) and extraction of clean whisper unaffected by noises for whispered data collection.

One of the main problems in the analysis of whispered speech is the limited amount of data available, and most specifically, spontaneous whispered speech.

A great source of whispered speech data are ASMR recordings available in streaming platforms like YouTube or Twitch.

We propose to train a WAD method on a synthetic noisy whispered dataset. The trained model is then used to detect whispered speech in ASMR recordings. Using Edyson to more accurately separate segments into "clean whisper" - "noisy whisper" - "noise".

The separated ASMR data is used for speech data augmentation and the proposed clean WAD (CWAD) model is trained using the specific ASMR triggers as noise.

ASMR data is used as an example of a great source of whispered speech available online. However, this framework could be adapted to other noisy whispered speech sources.
